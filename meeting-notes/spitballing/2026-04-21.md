program with attribution.

## Pitch 3: "What train am I on?" auto-detect

**PM:** Commuter PM
**Pitch:** When a user opens the app while moving, use GPS + recent
departure data to guess which specific train they boarded. One-tap
confirm to start a Live Activity.
**Why:** Current path to a Live Activity is open app → pick system →
pick route → pick train. Four taps, and the user already knows what
train they're on. Auto-detect makes Live Activities near-zero-friction
and could meaningfully shorten time-to-Pro-aha-moment.
**Rough effort:** M (GPS permission UX, matching logic, tunnel
fallback)
**Verdict:** pending

## Pitch 4: Next-departure home-screen widget

**PM:** Commuter PM
**Pitch:** iOS home-screen widget (Small + Medium) showing next
departure from a favorite station, auto-refreshing. Tap to open the
route view. Gate behind Pro.
**Why:** Widgets drive daily glances without opening the app, and
they're one of the clearest reasons to upgrade ("I use this on my
home screen every morning"). Natural companion to Live Activities —
same data model, same refresh logic.
**Rough effort:** M

**Pushback from Growth PM:** Widgets don't bring in *new* users. Every
engineering week not spent on reels or referrals is a week we miss the
10-sub goal. Defer to May.

**Pushback from Platform PM:** Widgets hammer the departures endpoint
on a timer. We don't have per-user quotas, and `route_history` and
`congestion_cache` are already known to be slow. Need to think about
cache sharing between widget and main app before we ship — otherwise
we 2–3x our read traffic overnight.

**Verdict:** pending

## Pitch 5: `JourneyStop.get_live_time()` helper

**PM:** Platform PM
**Pitch:** Add a `get_live_time()` method on the `JourneyStop` model
that returns `max(updated_departure, updated_arrival)` for NJT stops
and the appropriate single field for other providers. Grep call sites
and migrate them. Delete the warning comment once all consumers use
the helper.
**Why:** The CLAUDE.md has a prominent ⚠️ about NJT's inverted
`updated_arrival`/`updated_departure` semantics and says "Consumers
must use `max(updated_departure, updated_arrival)`." That's a
documented foot-gun. Someone will eventually forget `max()` and ship
a subtle delay-reporting bug. Wrap it once.
**Rough effort:** S (call-site migration + one unit test)
**Verdict:** pending
